Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 7, 2025

Summary: We do not collect, store, or track personal information. This service displays publicly available geographic data and does not use cookies or analytics.

1. Information We Do NOT Collect

The Mountain Cities Analysis service does NOT:

  • ❌ Collect personal information (name, email, phone, etc.)
  • ❌ Use cookies or tracking technologies
  • ❌ Use analytics or tracking scripts (Google Analytics, etc.)
  • ❌ Track your browsing behavior
  • ❌ Create user profiles
  • ❌ Collect location data beyond what your browser automatically sends
  • ❌ Store any data about your usage
  • ❌ Share data with third parties for marketing
  • ❌ Require login or user accounts

2. Standard Web Server Logs

Like all websites, the web server hosting these files may collect standard technical information in server logs:

  • IP Address: Your internet protocol address (automatically sent by your browser)
  • Timestamp: When you accessed the page
  • User Agent: Browser type and operating system
  • Referrer: Which page sent you here (if any)
  • Requested Files: Which HTML/JSON files were accessed

This is standard web server functionality and occurs for all websites. These logs are used solely for:

  • Technical troubleshooting
  • Security monitoring (detecting attacks)
  • Understanding aggregate usage patterns

Retention: Server logs (if kept) are typically retained for a limited period for troubleshooting purposes.

Not Shared: We do not sell, rent, or share server log data with third parties.

3. Third-Party Services

Map Tile Providers

When you view the maps, your browser loads map tiles from third-party providers (e.g., OpenTopoMap, OpenStreetMap). These providers may collect:

  • Your IP address
  • Which map tiles you requested (revealing approximate location you're viewing)

Why this happens: Map tiles are images loaded from external servers. When your browser requests these images, it sends standard HTTP request information.

Their policies: Tile providers have their own privacy policies. We do not control what they collect or how they use it.

Relevant policies:

  • OpenStreetMap Foundation Privacy Policy
  • OpenTopoMap About & Policies

External Links

Our maps include links to external websites (Google search, data source sites). When you click these links:

  • You leave our service and are subject to that website's privacy policy
  • Those sites may track your visit
  • We have no control over their practices

4. Browser Storage

Your browser may automatically cache files locally for performance:

  • HTML map files
  • JSON data files
  • Map tile images

This is standard browser behavior to make pages load faster. You can clear your browser cache at any time through your browser settings.

We do not use: LocalStorage, SessionStorage, IndexedDB, or other browser storage APIs to store data about you.

5. Data Displayed on Maps

All geographic data shown on the maps (cities, hospitals, airports, etc.) is:

  • ✅ Publicly available data from open sources
  • ✅ Not personal or private information
  • ✅ Not user-generated content

This data comes from public databases (GeoNames, OpenStreetMap, OurAirports) that are freely available to anyone. See our Attribution & Legal Information page for data sources.

6. Children's Privacy

This service does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Since we don't collect personal data at all, COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) concerns do not apply.

The service displays geographic information that is educational and appropriate for all ages.

7. International Users

This service is accessible worldwide. The data displayed covers mountain regions globally.

GDPR Compliance (EU Users):

  • We do not process personal data, so GDPR data processing requirements do not apply
  • Standard server logs (IP addresses) may be considered personal data under GDPR
  • Legal basis for server logs: Legitimate interest in security and technical operation
  • You have the right to request information about server logs (if kept)

Note: Third-party services (tile providers) may have different GDPR compliance approaches.

8. Your Privacy Rights

Since we don't collect personal information:

  • Access: There is no user data to access
  • Deletion: There is no user data to delete
  • Correction: There is no user data to correct
  • Portability: There is no user data to export
  • Opt-out: Nothing to opt out of

Browser Privacy: You can enhance your privacy by:

  • Using a VPN or proxy to mask your IP address
  • Using privacy-focused browsers or extensions
  • Disabling JavaScript (though maps won't work)
  • Clearing browser cache regularly

9. Security

Since we don't collect personal data, there is no personal data to protect. However:

  • This site should be served over HTTPS for secure transmission
  • We do not transmit sensitive information
  • All data is static and publicly available

If server logs are kept, they should be protected with appropriate technical and administrative safeguards.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date.

Material changes that would involve collecting personal information will be prominently announced.

Current policy: No personal data collection. This is unlikely to change, as the service is designed to be privacy-friendly.

11. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

For this service:

  • We do not sell personal information (we don't collect it)

If you believe we have collected personal information about you, you may contact us to request information.

12. "Do Not Track" Signals

Some browsers have "Do Not Track" (DNT) features. Since we don't track users at all, DNT signals are already honored by default.

Note: Third-party tile providers may not honor DNT signals - this is outside our control.
